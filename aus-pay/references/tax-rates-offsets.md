# Income tax rates and offsets — verified figures

Verified 15 July 2026 from ato.gov.au and Treasury Laws Amendment (More Cost of Living Relief) Act 2025 (No. 28, 2025 — assented 27 March 2025). Rates exclude the 2% Medicare levy.

## Resident rates

### FY 2025-26

| Taxable income | Tax on this income |
|---|---|
| $0 – $18,200 | Nil |
| $18,201 – $45,000 | 16c for each $1 over $18,200 |
| $45,001 – $135,000 | $4,288 plus 30c for each $1 over $45,000 |
| $135,001 – $190,000 | $31,288 plus 37c for each $1 over $135,000 |
| $190,001 and over | $51,638 plus 45c for each $1 over $190,000 |

### FY 2026-27 (legislated: 16% → 15%)

| Taxable income | Tax on this income |
|---|---|
| $0 – $18,200 | Nil |
| $18,201 – $45,000 | 15c for each $1 over $18,200 |
| $45,001 – $135,000 | $4,020 plus 30c for each $1 over $45,000 |
| $135,001 – $190,000 | $31,020 plus 37c for each $1 over $135,000 |
| $190,001 and over | $51,370 plus 45c for each $1 over $190,000 |

### FY 2027-28 (already law: 15% → 14%)

Same brackets; second rate 14%. Cumulative: $3,752 at $45,000; $30,752 at $135,000; $51,102 at $190,000.

## Foreign resident (non-resident) rates — same 2025-26 and 2026-27

| Taxable income | Tax on this income |
|---|---|
| $0 – $135,000 | 30c for each $1 |
| $135,001 – $190,000 | $40,500 plus 37c for each $1 over $135,000 |
| $190,001 and over | $60,850 plus 45c for each $1 over $190,000 |

No tax-free threshold. No Medicare levy. No LITO.

## Working holiday makers (417/462 visa) — same 2025-26 and 2026-27

| Taxable income | Tax on this income |
|---|---|
| $0 – $45,000 | 15c for each $1 |
| $45,001 – $135,000 | $6,750 plus 30c for each $1 over $45,000 |
| $135,001 – $190,000 | $33,750 plus 37c for each $1 over $135,000 |
| $190,001 and over | $54,100 plus 45c for each $1 over $190,000 |

Withholding: registered WHM employer withholds 15% up to $45,000 cumulative; unregistered employer withholds at foreign-resident rates; no TFN → 45% flat. WHM rates apply unless the worker is an Australian resident from a non-discriminatory-article (NDA) country (then resident rates).

## Low Income Tax Offset (LITO) — same both years, residents only

| Taxable income | LITO |
|---|---|
| $0 – $37,500 | $700 |
| $37,501 – $45,000 | $700 − 5c per $1 over $37,500 |
| $45,001 – $66,667 | $325 − 1.5c per $1 over $45,000 |
| $66,668+ | Nil |

Non-refundable; reduces income tax only (cannot reduce Medicare levy or MLS). LMITO is abolished (last year 2021-22).

## Seniors and Pensioners Tax Offset (SAPTO) — same both years

| Status | Max offset | Shade-out threshold (rebate income) | Cut-out |
|---|---|---|---|
| Single | $2,230 | $34,919 | $52,759 |
| Each partner of couple | $1,602 | $30,994 | $43,810 (combined $87,620) |
| Each partner, illness-separated | $2,040 | $33,732 | $50,052 |

Taper: 12.5c per $1 of rebate income above shade-out threshold. Based on "rebate income". Unused SAPTO transferable between spouses. Non-refundable.

## Tax-free threshold — withholding context

- $18,200/year ≈ $350/week, $700/fortnight, $1,517/month.
- Claimed on TFN declaration with one payer (usually highest-paying). Second job normally uses "no tax-free threshold" withholding column.
- Part-year resident threshold: $13,464 + up to $4,736 pro-rated by months in Australia (incl. arrival month).
- No TFN provided within 28 days → withhold 47% (resident) / 45% (foreign resident), no offsets.

## Other offsets (active, parameters not re-verified this pass)

Private health insurance rebate (income-tested, refundable), zone tax offset, overseas forces offset, invalid/invalid-carer offset, spouse super contribution offset (max $540), super income stream offset, small business income tax offset (max $1,000).
