# Medicare levy, MLS, HELP/STSL, Superannuation — verified figures

All figures verified directly from ato.gov.au as at 15 July 2026.

## Medicare levy

**Rate: 2% of taxable income** (FY 2025-26 and FY 2026-27).

### Low-income reduction thresholds — FY 2025-26

| Category | Lower threshold (no levy at/below) | Upper threshold (reduced levy at/below) |
|---|---|---|
| Single, non-SAPTO | $28,011 | $35,013 |
| Single, SAPTO-eligible | $44,268 | $55,335 |
| Family, non-SAPTO | $47,238 | $59,047 |
| Family, SAPTO-eligible | $61,623 | $77,028 |

- Per dependent child: lower family threshold +$4,338; upper family threshold +$5,423.
- Shade-in: between lower and upper thresholds, levy = **10c per $1 over the lower threshold** (ATO worked example: taxable income $29,000 → levy $98.90 = 10% × ($29,000 − $28,011)). Above upper threshold, full 2% applies.
- Levy is min(2% × taxable income, 10% × (taxable income − lower threshold)), floored at 0.
- SAPTO note: SAPTO single entitlement ceases at rebate income $52,759; if SAPTO reduces to zero before the family upper limit, use non-SAPTO thresholds.

**FY 2026-27 low-income thresholds: not yet published** (legislated retrospectively each year, typically in the Budget after year end). Use 2025-26 values as provisional and flag as such in output.

### Medicare levy exemptions

| Exemption | Category | Conditions |
|---|---|---|
| Medical | 1 | Full or half exemption for certain medical conditions (blind pensioners, etc.) |
| Foreign resident | 2 | Full exemption for the period of foreign residency |
| Not entitled to Medicare | 3 | Requires Medicare Entitlement Statement (MES) from Services Australia — e.g. many temporary visa holders (482 etc.). Denied if a dependant/spouse is Medicare-entitled and not exempt |
| Diplomatic/consular | — | Not an Australian citizen, not ordinarily resident |

Half exemption = 1% levy instead of 2%.

## Medicare levy surcharge (MLS)

Applies only if no adequate private hospital cover AND income for MLS purposes exceeds the base tier. In addition to the 2% levy.

### FY 2025-26

| | Base tier | Tier 1 | Tier 2 | Tier 3 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Single | ≤ $101,000 | $101,001–$118,000 | $118,001–$158,000 | ≥ $158,001 |
| Family | ≤ $202,000 | $202,001–$236,000 | $236,001–$316,000 | ≥ $316,001 |
| Rate | 0% | 1% | 1.25% | 1.5% |

### FY 2026-27

| | Base tier | Tier 1 | Tier 2 | Tier 3 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Single | ≤ $105,000 | $105,001–$123,000 | $123,001–$164,000 | ≥ $164,001 |
| Family | ≤ $210,000 | $210,001–$246,000 | $246,001–$328,000 | ≥ $328,001 |
| Rate | 0% | 1% | 1.25% | 1.5% |

- Family threshold: +$1,500 for each MLS dependent child after the first.
- **Income for MLS purposes** (you + spouse): taxable income (excl. assessable FHSS released amounts) + reportable fringe benefits (grossed-up reported amount) + total net investment losses + reportable super contributions + spouse's s98 trust income + exempt foreign employment income.
- Tier is determined by full MLS income, but the surcharge is **charged on taxable income + reportable fringe benefits** only (ATO example: $90,000 taxable + $27,000 RFB → MLS = 1% × $117,000).

## HELP / study and training support loans (STSL)

Marginal repayment system from 2025-26 onwards. Same thresholds/rates for ALL loan types: HELP, VSL, SFSS, SSL, ABSTUDY SSL, AASL (formerly TSL). Repayments applied in order: HELP → VSL → SFSS → SSL → ABSTUDY SSL → AASL.

### FY 2025-26

| Repayment income | Repayment |
|---|---|
| $0 – $67,000 | Nil |
| $67,001 – $125,000 | 15c for each $1 over $67,000 |
| $125,001 – $179,285 | $8,700 + 17c for each $1 over $125,000 |
| $179,286 and over | **10% of TOTAL repayment income** (flat, not marginal) |

### FY 2026-27 (indexed to AWE)

| Repayment income | Repayment |
|---|---|
| $0 – $69,528 | Nil |
| $69,529 – $129,717 | 15c for each $1 over $69,528 |
| $129,718 – $186,050 | $9,028 + 17c for each $1 over $129,717 |
| $186,051 and over | **10% of TOTAL repayment income** (flat, not marginal) |

- **Repayment income** = taxable income (excl. assessable FHSS amounts) + reportable fringe benefits (regardless of employer FBT-exempt status) + total net investment loss (incl. net rental losses) + reportable super contributions + exempt foreign employment income.
- Repayment is capped at the outstanding loan balance.
- The one-off 20% debt reduction (balances as at 1 June 2025) is complete and affects loan balances only — it does NOT change any pay calculation.

### STSL withholding (Schedule 8) — payments from 1 July 2026

STSL withholding is a separate component added on top of standard PAYG. Formula y = a×x − b, where x = weekly earnings rounded down to whole dollar + $0.99.

Tax-free threshold claimed or foreign resident:

| Weekly earnings x | a | b |
|---|---|---|
| < $1,337 | 0.00 | 0 |
| < $2,494 | 0.15 | 200.5615 |
| < $3,577 | 0.17 | 250.4527 |
| ≥ $3,577 | 0.10 | 0 |

No tax-free threshold claimed:

| Weekly earnings x | a | b |
|---|---|---|
| < $987 | 0.00 | 0 |
| < $2,144 | 0.15 | 148.0615 |
| < $2,727 | 0.17 | 190.9527 |
| ≥ $2,727 | 0.10 | 0 |

No STSL component if employee lodged a Medicare levy variation declaration claiming low-family-income reduction/exemption.

Note: STSL withholding is computed on taxable earnings only; reportable super add-back happens at assessment — heavy salary sacrifice makes withholding understate the final compulsory repayment.

## Superannuation

| Item | FY 2025-26 | FY 2026-27 |
|---|---|---|
| Super guarantee (SG) rate | 12.00% | 12.00% (final legislated rate) |
| Maximum contribution base | $62,500 per **quarter** (max SG $7,500/qtr) | $270,830 per **year** (Payday Super — annual base = concessional cap × 100 ÷ 12) |
| Concessional contributions cap | $30,000 | $32,500 (AWOTE-indexed) |
| Division 293 threshold | $250,000 (not indexed) | $250,000 |
| Division 293 rate | extra 15% on concessional contributions above threshold | same |

**Payday Super (from 1 July 2026)**: SG paid per payday on "qualifying earnings" (was ordinary time earnings, quarterly). Once qualifying earnings in a financial year reach $270,830, SG stops for the rest of that year.

### Salary-sacrificed super — effects

| Base | Effect |
|---|---|
| Taxable income / PAYG withholding | Reduced (not assessable, not withheld on) |
| SG earnings base | NOT reduced — employer pays full SG as if no arrangement; sacrificed amounts don't count toward employer SG obligation |
| HELP repayment income | Added back (reportable employer super contributions) |
| MLS income | Added back |
| Div 293 income | Added back (counts as concessional contributions vs $250k threshold) |
| Concessional cap | Counts toward cap; taxed 15% in fund |
| FBT | Not a fringe benefit if paid to complying super fund |
